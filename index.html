Ivan Yakovlev

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn.

I have defended my PhD thesis entitled "Metric ribbon graphs" in July 2024 at LaBRI in Bordeaux, where my supervisor was Vincent Delecroix. I was a member of the Combinatorics and Interactions team.

Before that I obtained my Master's degree from École Normale Supérieure and Sorbonne Université in Paris.

Research interests

I am interested in enumerative problems related to surfaces and their moduli spaces. Some keywords: hyperbolic / translation / square-tiled surfaces, Masur--Veech volumes, combinatorial maps (\(=\) ribbon graphs), intersection theory on \(\mathcal{M}_{g,n}\), Hurwitz numbers.

I am also interested in mapping class groups, algorithmic problems on surfaces, integer polytopes and I enjoy experimenting in SageMath.
